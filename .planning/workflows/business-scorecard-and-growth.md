## Core scorecard

Track these every week, review them every month, and change targets quarterly.

| KPI | Formula | Why it matters |
|-----|---------|----------------|
| **Revenue** | Closed revenue in the month | Tells you if the business is actually moving |
| **Projected revenue** | Open quotes weighted by close probability | Prevents reactive hiring and equipment buys |
| **MRR** | Active maintenance-plan revenue per month | Stabilizes cash flow and route density |
| **Gross profit** | Revenue - direct labor - chemicals - fuel - card fees - subcontractors | Shows if jobs are worth doing |
| **Gross margin** | Gross profit / revenue | Reveals pricing mistakes and underbidding |
| **Quote win rate** | Accepted quotes / sent quotes | Exposes weak sales process or bad pricing |
| **Revenue per labor hour** | Revenue / sold labor hours | Best simple productivity metric for field work |
| **Repeat rate** | Repeat customers / active customers | Indicates retention and future MRR potential |
| **Average ticket** | Revenue / completed jobs | Helps decide whether bigger-area work is paying off |

## Minimum scorecard review rules

- If **quote win rate is low** and **gross margin is healthy**, sales/follow-up is the issue.
- If **quote win rate is high** and **gross margin is weak**, pricing is too low.
- If **revenue is growing** but **revenue per labor hour is flat**, the business is adding effort without leverage.
- If **MRR is flat**, the team is closing jobs but not building a maintenance base.

## Losses and liabilities to track

Treat these as part of the dashboard, not as end-of-month surprises.

- **Sales tax payable**: tax collected and not yet remitted
- **Processing fees and refunds**
- **Debt obligations**: washer, trailer, vehicle, or tank financing
- **Repair reserve**: pump, hose, fitting, tire, and trailer maintenance
- **Warranty / callback reserve**: rework time and damage claims
- **Payroll taxes / contractor payouts**
- **Accounts payable**: chemicals, fuel, repairs, suppliers

## MRR model for this business

Grime Time should not expect MRR to appear automatically. Create it deliberately.

Offer recurring plans like:

- Quarterly house wash checkup
- Biannual driveway / flatwork refresh
- Storefront / HOA exterior touch-up schedule
- Gutter or window maintenance add-on

Track:

- Active plan count
- Monthly plan revenue
- Churned plans
- Upsells from maintenance customers into larger cleanings

## Daily operating rhythm

Default rhythm unless weather, access, or customer constraints force a change:

| Time | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **7:30-8:00** | Review weather, route, open quotes, unpaid invoices, and tomorrow schedule |
| **8:00-9:30** | Calls, texts, quote follow-up, CRM cleanup, review requests, customer relationship work |
| **9:30-12:30** | First production block |
| **12:30-1:00** | Lunch + confirm afternoon arrival windows |
| **1:00-4:30** | Second production block |
| **4:30-5:15** | Photos, invoices, payments, notes, tomorrow confirmations |

Working rule:

- Put **sales and relationship work first** unless a job is already staged and time-sensitive.
- Prefer **jobs in the morning or afternoon blocks**, not randomly scattered all day.
- Stack nearby jobs together to create route density.

## Weekly and monthly rhythm

### Weekly

- **Monday:** pipeline review, schedule review, quote backlog cleanup
- **Wednesday:** pricing review, callback review, route-density check
- **Friday:** bookkeeping, review requests, maintenance-plan follow-up, next-week prep

### Monthly

- Review scorecard and compare actuals vs targets
- Adjust pricing only after margin + win-rate review
- Decide whether the next asset purchase is justified by bottlenecks, not by impulse

## Growth ladder: make it feel like a game

Each stage unlocks the next one.

| Stage | Trigger | Win condition |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| **1. Reliable base** | `0-8 jobs/month` | Quotes go out quickly, photos are captured, bookkeeping is current |
| **2. Route density** | `8-20 jobs/month` | Jobs are grouped geographically, fewer dead miles, repeat offers begin |
| **3. Systemized sales** | `20-35 jobs/month` | Same-day quotes, templated follow-up, review requests become routine |
| **4. Crew leverage** | `35+ jobs/month` or owner bottleneck | SOPs exist, owner is not the only field operator or closer |
| **5. Commercial push** | steady large-area demand | Equipment and systems support higher-ticket flatwork / commercial accounts |

## Asset ladder

Buy assets to remove the current bottleneck. Do not buy prestige equipment before the work exists.

### 1. Low-cost speed upgrades

Buy first:

- Better hose setup
- Spare tips, o-rings, fittings, repair kit
- Downstream chemical injector
- Cones / caution signs
- Branded yard sign or truck magnet

Why:

- This is the cheapest way to cut setup friction and look more professional.

### 2. Flatwork production upgrade

Buy when driveways, sidewalks, patios, and larger concrete areas are becoming frequent:

- **20-inch commercial surface cleaner**
- **150-200 ft hose reel setup**

Current product references:

- Landa’s **Cyclone Surface Cleaner** is a 20-inch unit for sidewalks, pool decks, parking lots, and other flat surfaces: https://www.landa.com/en/accessories/cyclone-surface-cleaner-89036080.html
- BE Power Equipment’s **200' Hose Reel with Hand Crank** is rated for 200 feet of 3/8" hose at 5,000 PSI: https://bepowerequipment.com/products/85-402-002

Operational reading:

- This is the highest-probability upgrade for cleaning larger areas faster with the washer you already have.

### 3. Water independence

Buy when weak water supply, long hose runs, or remote properties are costing time:

- **300-325 gallon buffer tank**
- Trailer or truck-bed mounting plan

Current product reference:

- Snyder Industries’ **325-gallon water transport utility tank** is designed for bed transport and uses polyethylene construction: https://www.northerntool.com/products/snyder-industries-water-transport-utility-tank-325-gallon-capacity-model-1021700w94301-48693

### 4. Throughput upgrade

Buy only after sales are proven and the current machine is the bottleneck:

- Move to a **truck- or trailer-mount 8+ GPM unit**

Current product reference:

- BE Power Equipment lists a **4,200 PSI / 8.5 GPM** truck-mount unit for large-scale commercial and industrial cleaning: https://bepowerequipment.com/products/b4224hetbas-cold-water-gas-pressure-washer

Interpretation:

- For larger areas, **GPM** is the real speed lever. PSI matters, but volume is what makes concrete and commercial jobs faster.

### 5. Premium / specialty work

Only after the core business is stable:

- Hot-water capability
- Dedicated soft-wash / chemical workflow

Current product references:

- General Pump’s downstream injector sheet notes the injector is for chemical application and requires low pressure to activate flow: https://www.generalpump.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/10/Y21015421-Chem-Inj.pdf
- NorthStar’s commercial trailer systems show how tank + trailer + hot-water packages scale into remote commercial work: https://www.northerntool.com/products/northstar-proshot-hot-water-commercial-pressure-washer-trailer-4000-psi-5-5-gpm-kubota-diesel-engine-400-gal-water-tank-157569%3Fsrsltid%3DAfmBOopboVZzVgWkphGoEuxl5pQFBnwMRyhHc8BVNumyPqQKH7HmvV2A

## Recommended software stack

Keep the stack boring and operational.

### 1. Field service / quoting

**Jobber** is the best next-step recommendation if you want a unified quoting, scheduling, and approval flow.

Why:

- Jobber says customers can approve and request changes online in client hub.
- Jobber also says quotes can show estimated margin, support text/email delivery, and automate follow-ups.

Sources:

- https://www.getjobber.com/features/quotes/
- https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500011237822-What-Do-Your-Clients-See-in-Client-Hub-

### 2. Job photo proof

**CompanyCam** is a good fit when photo documentation starts affecting trust, rework, or crew accountability.

Why:

- CompanyCam markets photo documentation, markups, and shareable before/after reports for contractors.

Source:

- https://companycam.com/photo-documentation

### 3. Accounting / profitability

**QuickBooks job costing** is the simplest recommendation for revenue, margin, and estimate-vs-actual tracking.

Why:

- QuickBooks says the level of detail for job costing can be tuned to the business, which fits an operation that is still evolving.

Source:

- https://quickbooks.intuit.com/accounting/job-costing/

## Edit rules as the business scales

- Keep formulas stable; change **targets**, not definitions, unless the business model changes.
- Review milestones every 90 days.
- Only add a new metric if someone will act on it every week.
- Only buy an asset when it removes a measurable bottleneck.

## Immediate recommendation

If the business already owns a pressure washer, the next likely purchases should be:

1. **Surface cleaner**
2. **Longer hose + reel**
3. **Buffer tank**
4. **Only then** consider a bigger GPM unit

That order is pragmatic because it improves speed, reach, and job size without jumping straight into a heavy capital purchase.
