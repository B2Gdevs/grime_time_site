# Method Artifacts SOPs

Phase `14-06` formalizes the first crew-training artifacts that should exist before deeper quoting or automation starts implying execution confidence. Each artifact becomes:

- a one-page field checklist
- a short photo or diagram guide
- a supervisor-led walkthrough
- a pass or fail sign-off sequence in admin or the operations runbook

Train every relevant role (production, detail, soft-wash) against the same checklist before they touch the wand without supervision.

## Artifact checklists

## Soft Wash Core

### Required setup

- Confirm surface type supports soft-wash chemistry.
- Verify downstream mix target and rinse source before spraying.
- Mask or pre-wet sensitive plants, outlets, and fixtures.

### Pass criteria

- Organics visibly die off without oxidation, striping, or fan-marking.
- Rinse path stays top to bottom.
- Operator can explain dwell-time adjustment when sun or wind changes.

### Fail flags

- Letting solution dry on the surface.
- Random spot rinsing that leaves streak lines.
- No plant or fixture protection on a risk surface.

## Controlled Pressure Wash

### Required setup

- Confirm PSI and nozzle lane for the surface before trigger pull.
- Walk the surface for cracks, loose coating, and edge-spall risk.
- Establish a consistent stand-off distance.

### Pass criteria

- Wand motion stays even with no zebra striping or gouge marks.
- Edges and transitions are cleaned with reduced aggression.
- Operator can explain when to downgrade to soft wash or manual review.

### Fail flags

- Stopping the wand in one spot.
- Blasting expansion joints, chipped edges, or decorative finish.
- Treating every concrete surface as the same pressure lane.

## Surface Cleaner Pass

### Required setup

- Confirm deck size matches the working lane and water flow.
- Start at a known edge and map overlap direction.
- Plan wand cleanup passes for borders and corners.

### Pass criteria

- Overlap is deliberate and leaves no dirty stripes.
- Walking pace stays consistent across the lane.
- Cleanup pass matches the main deck finish.

### Fail flags

- Wandering lane lines or missed edge cleanup.
- Fast or slow walking that creates visible pass variation.
- Using the surface cleaner where access or obstacles require another method.

## Organic Displacement

### Required setup

- Explain to the crew that webs relocate before they disappear.
- Identify likely reattachment points under rails, joists, and corners.
- Choose a spray direction that moves webs away from finished areas.

### Pass criteria

- Crew follows one directional displacement plan instead of random spraying.
- Recheck points are revisited after the first pass.
- Operator can describe when webs signal a repeat-clean maintenance candidate.

### Fail flags

- Random cross-spraying that redistributes webs everywhere.
- Treating the first displacement as final.
- No callback plan for heavy organic return zones.

## Formal training sequence

Each crew member sees the same micro-course:

1. Review the artifact checklist and safety flags before field practice.
2. Run guided practice with a supervisor on at least two real surface examples per artifact.
3. Complete a short troubleshooting quiz:
   - what changes when the surface is more delicate than expected?
   - what makes the lane manual-review-only?
   - what visual mistake means the pass failed?
4. Supervisor signs off only when the operator can explain and demonstrate the lane cleanly.

## Recording completion

- Track completion against the employee or crew profile once training records land in admin.
- Until then, log completion in the operations runbook and mark task `14-06` complete in planning.
