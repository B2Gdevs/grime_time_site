# Field quoting and revisions (staff)

## How we sell today

Most new business still comes from **door-to-door and on-site conversations**. Reps carry the relationship: qualify the property, build trust, and close with a price the homeowner can say yes to.

## What the product is adding

- **Website capture** — Forms and instant quote flows feed the same first-party CRM as field work, so nothing lives in a separate “marketing CRM.”
- **On-site digital quotes** — Use the internal quote tools in front of the customer, send the proposal to their email or phone immediately, and keep the conversation moving while you are still at the door or on the driveway.
- **Adjustments when the first price does not stick** — If they push back on scope or price, **create another quote** for the **same email or phone / contact identity**. Multiple quotes per contact are expected while you negotiate.
- **Which one “won”** — After the job is completed (or the opportunity is clearly lost), operations reconcile **which quote or opportunity actually closed** against the work billed. The system should treat duplicate quotes as versions of the same sale, not as unrelated leads, whenever the contact matches.

## CRM alignment

- **Leads and opportunities** from the field and from the site should land in the **Ops workspace** queues so follow-up does not depend on memory.
- **Commercial and residential** accounts may share the same portal patterns; company-level context belongs on the account record when you are ready to model it fully.
